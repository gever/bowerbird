Bowerbird: Automated Retrieve Tracking & Communication

Automated Retrieve Tracking & Communication “Bowerbird” for Applegate Open 2021

NOTE: Thanks to Bernie Nowel (a participant at AO 2019) there are customized instructions in Bowerbird for setting up your Inreach/SPOT!

SETTING UP YOUR INREACH / SPOT

Your Pilot Number can be found on the event website in the first (“p-number”) column:

http://wingsoverapplegate.org/pilots

1 - Go to http://bbtrack.me

2 - Locate the tile with your Pilot Number

3 - Click on your tile and confirm it’s yours (your name will be shown at the top)

4 - Click on the link “Tracking Device Setup”

5 - Follow the instructions, including testing all three of your messages (LOK, PUP, AID).

NOTE: Do NOT test your messages after the competition has begun, unless instructed to by the organization staff or volunteers.

PILOT TRACKING

Pilots flying this event will be tracked using the automated Bowerbird system. This means that all pilots will be able to confirm their own pilot status online. You are responsible for confirming that your status is green at the end of each day using

http://bbtrack.me or checking the board in HQ. (This is required for SAFETY reasons, not just retrieve.)

The first step to being tracked is to configure your tracking device exactly as described in “SETTING UP INREACH / SPOT” above.

The second step is to save these numbers for communicating with Retrieve:

  • SMS ONLY: +1 415-213-4242 for sending status information (automatically logged and acknowledged via Bowerbird - these must have the proper format!)
  • Voice: +1 541-816-9072 for calling if you require assistance or can’t communicate on the Retrieve radio frequency

Finally, be sure to follow the tracking protocols (described in detail below):

  1. Check-out on launch (at the “Check out Gate”)
  2. Send LOK message on landing
  3. Send PUP message when you’ve been picked up
  4. Confirm you are green on the Bowerbird board
  5. Upload your track log or turn your GPS in to the scorekeepers
  6. Enjoy your evening!
  7. Confirm that your track scored as you expected by looking at the results the following day

LAUNCH PROTOCOL

BEFORE LAUNCH:

  1. Turn on your satellite tracking device (SPOT or InReach).
  2. Turn on tracking.
  3. Turn on your radio and tune to the primary meet frequency.
  4. Go through the “check out gate”: you will be checked out to fly and your equipment set up will be confirmed.

Do not enter the “check out gate” until you are ready to launch (all geared up).

Super Clinic or Volunteer? Please read these Special Instructions for your protocol: http://bit.ly/bb-special-instructions

LANDING PROTOCOL

(This assumes you have already followed the steps in “SETTING UP INREACH / SPOT” above!)

Please land on or by the most major road that you can for quick retrieve.  Any pilot that lands away from a paved road is expected to walk to a major road in order to be picked up.  

 

ON LANDING:

IF YOU ARE OK (LOK):

Immediately upon landing, rosette your wing so that any pilots above you know you Landed OK. Then, use your InReach / SPOT to send your #1 (LOK) Message. This way the organizers know that you are no longer flying and are safely on the ground. Switch your radio to the RETRIEVE Frequency (151.925); pack your gear and head to the closest road.

Monitor the radio for a call from a driver that will be looking for you. DO NOT TURN YOUR RADIO OR TRACKING DEVICE OFF UNTIL YOU ARE IN A RETRIEVE VEHICLE AND HAVE SENT YOUR PICKED-UP (PUP) MESSAGE. If there are issues with retrieve call the Retrieve Coordinator 541-930-1404.

If you had to hike to reach a main retrieve road, send your LOK message AGAIN. This will confirm to retrieve exactly where you are and that you are ready to be picked up.

 

If you hitch a ride back to HQ, or a friend picks you up (an unofficial retrieve), be sure to send your #3 message (PUP) indicating that you have been Picked UP. If you don’t have a #3/Custom message on your tracking device, you can TEXT your PUP message using this format:

#000 PUP Pete Pepper

Send this text message to: +1 415-213-4242

IF YOU NEED HELP BUT IT IS NOT AN EMERGENCY:

Send your #2/HELP preset message (AID) asking for assistance.

Call the Safety Director 1-808-772-8551 if you are able and provide the details of your situation. Pack up your wing if you can. Wait for retrieval.

 

Remember: pilots above will be watching to see if you move and will be reporting.  If you have made contact with HQ/Safety officials and can gather your equipment - in particular, rosette your wing - please do so.  That will show the pilots above that you are not in need of emergency services.

 

IF YOU ARE SERIOUSLY INJURED:

Leave your wing open as a sign to other pilots that you are SERIOUSLY injured.

Press the “SOS” button (for SPOT hold until it starts to blink; for InReach slide lock to left to unlock and then press and hold button for 3 seconds).  

 

Do not press the “SOS” button a second time on SPOT units - this will cancel the 911 transmission. To cancel an InReach SOS, press and hold button for 5 seconds.

 

Call 911 on your telephone if you are able.

Call the Safety Director 1-808-772-8551 if you are able.

Do not move. Do not get out of your harness. Wait for assistance.

“CHECK IN” PROTOCOL 

Only check in once you are in a retrieve vehicle or at AO HQ.

CHECK IN (PUP):

There are four ways you can “check in” after flying (PUP = Picked UP):

  1. Send your PUP message using your InReach or SPOT.
  2. Send an SMS message

To: +1 415-213-4242

Message: #000 PUP Pete Pepper

  1. Someone else in your retrieve vehicle may be able to send a PUP message for you
  2. At HQ ask one of the retrieve monitors to send a PUP message for you

PUP messages are acknowledged by the system immediately, and the Bowerbird board updates within seconds. If your status is NOT green on the Bowerbird board, then send another PUP message. Make sure your 2021 Applegate Open pilot number is green (it’s probably not the same as last year’s Applegate Open pilot number :-).

CONFIRM YOU ARE CHECKED IN:

Pilots are required to confirm they are checked in.  Remember, it is YOUR responsibility to ensure we know you are safe and sound. The organizers are using this system to make things easier and to avoid unneeded Search & Rescue efforts for pilots who are happily drinking a beer somewhere.

Your status messages will be displayed on the Bowerbird Pilot Overview page: http://bbtrack.me

or your individual pilot page: http://bbtrack.me/pilot/0000

You can easily check your own status via the website or on the big display at HQ.

Before the competition starts there will likely be various status indicators while testing.

 

From the Local Rules for the Applegate Open:

Failure to check in by the report time will result in the pilot losing a portion or all of their daily score on the first offense depending on the circumstance.  This will be subject to the meet director’s discretion.  Pilots who fail to report will also be responsible financially for any search and rescue expense mounted on their behalf.  

Pilots who have a second reporting offense can expect to be removed from the competition without a refund.

UPLOADING TRACK LOGS:

During registration, you will get a unique pilot code from the scorekeepers for use in uploading your track log (it will be on your badge).

  1. Connect your flight instrument to your computer (or make sure your tracklog is on your computer)
  2. Go to http://trackupload.paraglide.us
  3. Enter your unique pilot code             
  4. You should see your name (if not, try again or check with the scorekeepers)
  5. Click the “Browse” button and select the IGC tracklog for your flight
  6. Once the track log has been saved in the database, you can the View track

 

Be sure to View the track - if the track is not the correct one, upload another. The scoring software will use the last uploaded tracklog.

NOTE: Your pilot code for track upload is different from your pilot number.

Uploading a track log is NOT a substitute for checking-in (sending your PUP message).

After you’ve checked in and submitted your track log you’re all done - enjoy your evening!
